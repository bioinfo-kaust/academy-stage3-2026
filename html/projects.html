Group Projects: Chromosome-specific TDP-43 Knockout Analysis

Project Goal

Each group will analyze the effect of TDP-43 knockout on gene expression in their assigned chromosome. Using the same experimental dataset (GSE136366) from the labs, you will apply the complete RNA-seq analysis pipeline to discover chromosome-specific impacts of TDP-43 loss.

Project Overview

Background

TDP-43 is a ubiquitously expressed RNA-binding protein that regulates thousands of target genes across the genome. While the labs focused on chromosome 11, TDP-43's effects are distributed across all chromosomes. Your challenge is to identify:

  • Which genes on your chromosome are affected by TDP-43 knockout
  • What biological processes are disrupted
  • Whether your chromosome has unique or particularly strong responses to TDP-43 loss
  • Potential links to ALS/FTD pathology based on affected genes

Tips for Success

Start Early & Team Work

The computational pipeline takes time. Start your analysis as soon as possible so you have time to interpret results and prepare your presentation.

Compare with Literature

Search PubMed for your top DE genes + "TDP-43" to see if they've been reported before. This adds credibility and context to your findings.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid
  • Not checking mapping rates (low rates indicate problems)
  • Forgetting to use the correct chromosome-specific reference files
  • Over-interpreting results from a small number of DE genes
